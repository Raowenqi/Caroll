Camera Fundamentals

Exposure

exposure and exposure three elements refer to aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity ISO;
Exposure is simply the brightness of our picture light, too bright is overexposure, also known as overexposure, too dark is underexposure, also known as underexposure;
There are relevant light meter data can be referred to.

Aperture

aperture, is the size of the aperture of the light into the camera, the larger the aperture (the smaller the value), the larger the light aperture, the more light into the amount of light, so the greater the exposure, the higher the brightness of the picture, the shallower the depth of field, the higher the degree of background blur (the background is fuzzy), and vice versa.

Shutter Speed

shutter speed refers to the length of time that light enters the camera, it refers to the shutter opening to close this period of time, the faster the shutter speed, the shorter the light entering time, the less light entering, the smaller the exposure, the darker the picture, and vice versa.

Sensitivity ISO

sensitivity ISO, sensitivity is the sensitivity of the sensor to light, the same light brightness, the higher the sensitivity, the more sensitive the sensor to light, the brighter the picture, high sensitivity will produce noise, it is generally recommended to use with caution.

White Balance

white balance, simply put, is the setting of the color temperature K value of the camera, the general K value is calculated as the middle value in 5000 as the benchmark: the picture is blue, when the cold tone is displayed, the K value is lowered to 5000;
The screen is yellow, when the warm tone is displayed, the K value is adjusted to more than 5000.
